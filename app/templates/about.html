About Hawaiʻi Cats

Hawaiʻi Cats is dedicated to understanding and managing feral cat populations in the Hawaiian Islands through advanced tools, community engagement, and scientific research. Our mission is to provide actionable insights for effective colony management while supporting the preservation of Hawaiʻi’s native ecosystems.

Our Mission

Hawaiʻi faces a unique challenge with feral cats. Their presence impacts native wildlife, including endangered species, and places a financial burden on management efforts. Hawaiʻi Cats bridges the gap between conservation, community needs, and cost-effective solutions by offering data-driven tools and fostering collaboration.

Our Tools

Our tools are designed to empower users with accurate data and actionable insights:

  • Population Estimator: An interactive modeling tool to simulate and predict feral cat population trends under different scenarios. Users can adjust parameters such as reproduction rates, survival probabilities, and mortality risks to better understand long-term outcomes.
  • Cost Calculator: Built into the Population Estimator, this feature helps estimate the financial costs of managing a colony over time, considering factors like sterilization, healthcare, feeding, and other resources.
  • CatMap: A geographic tool that visualizes cat colonies and highlights areas of conservation interest or management priority. Users can assess colony size, growth rates, and potential environmental impacts.
  • Data Export: Both tools allow for exporting data, enabling further analysis or use in presentations and reports.

How It Works

Hawaiʻi Cats tools provide a user-friendly interface for analyzing colony dynamics and management costs. The Population Estimator models real-world scenarios, while CatMap offers visual insights into colony locations and potential ecological impacts.

Beta Release & Community Feedback

These tools are in active development. While they are grounded in the best available data, feedback from users is crucial to refining their accuracy and usability. We encourage biologists, conservationists, and community members to share their experiences and suggestions.

Future Vision

Hawaiʻi Cats is committed to expanding its capabilities with advanced analytics and scientific integration. Planned future developments include:

  • Mapping Toxoplasmosis Prevalence: Using sighting data and individual cat testing for toxoplasma antibodies to create geographic maps of toxoplasmosis prevalence. This will provide insights into hotspots and inform public health and conservation strategies.
  • Machine Learning for Carrying Capacity: Leveraging machine learning algorithms to understand geographic carrying capacity across different regions and improve the estimation of impacts caused by colony feeders. This analysis will help in predicting resource strain and planning management interventions.
  • Enhanced Predictive Analytics: Integration of predictive modeling to identify high-risk areas for population surges and their ecological impacts, helping prioritize management actions.
  • Customizable Dashboards: Future updates will include user-friendly dashboards for monitoring colony dynamics, analyzing costs, and visualizing the interaction between colonies and sensitive ecosystems.

Who We Serve

Hawaiʻi Cats is built for:

  • Conservationists managing native ecosystems

  • Researchers studying population dynamics and disease prevalence

  • Colony managers assessing resources and outcomes

  • Community members contributing to conservation and management efforts
